Mana

Mana is neither Matter, Darkmatter nor Antimatter — it is something else entirely. Like Antimatter, it is a negative thing. Mana itself is invisible to the naked eye; what can be perceived is only the pale, faint yellow glow that surrounds it: a dim aura produced as a passive byproduct of Antimatter continuously converting into Matter (and vice versa) in its presence. Mana carries no mass, no weight, and has no known minimum size. It is buoyancy-neutral — it drifts freely, present everywhere and bound by nothing except the quantum spaces, in which mana is created, like everything else.

Mana can transform Antimatter into any form of Matter and reverse that process at will. It can also rearrange existing Matter into new forms, and impart attributes upon it. Magic

Beings with a soul are capable of casting Magic through Mana. When a spell works purely by sending attributes to an object — rather than reshaping Matter — a beam of Mana travels toward the target. The color of the beam reflects the intent of the spell. The beam persists until the attributes have been fully integrated into the object, or until the caster deliberately stops it.

Methods of Casting

There are three recognized ways to cast Magic:

  1. Thought Magic: The most demanding form. The caster visualizes the desired spell entirely in their mind, and the soul handles the rest. It demands a strong visual imagination, high intelligence, and some practice — though the basics are not hard to grasp. The more complex the spell, the more precise and demanding the visualization must be.
  2. Thought and Buzzword Magic: The standard method of casting. The caster visualizes the spell while speaking words aloud to anchor and assist the process. These words need not be literally related to the spell — though in practice, using relevant words tends to make casting easier and more reliable.
  3. Written Magic: Reserved for magical traps and for individuals entirely without inner thought — be it visual, auditory, or otherwise. The caster must first achieve a thorough, technical understanding of how the soul translates magical intent into Mana instructions. The complete spell must then be written out in full, with every detail specified. A simple spell may take hours to transcribe; complex ones can take days or weeks.

Mana Levels and Spell Cost

Both the Mana in the soul and the Mana in the body are measured in Levels, where 1 cm³ equals one Level. There is no upper limit to Levels, but there are physical thresholds that can only be raised through sustained mental and physical training.

The cost of casting a spell is determined by the following formula:

Cost (Levels) = Volume of the spell (in cm³) × sum of attribute multipliers * hours

This formula allows a caster to determine exactly how many spells of a given type they can cast from their current reserves, and whether a particular cast is safe to attempt. Attempting a spell without sufficient Mana results in an incomplete cast — which can have catastrophic consequences. For example: insufficient Mana during an atomic-scale manipulation could trigger an uncontrolled split, or cause a spell component to discharge prematurely. In most spells, the hours multiplier is calculated by the soul and brain and therefore not relevant. That is mostly the case in attack spells like fireball, where the spell only lasts so long.

Body Enhancements

The Mana permeating a being's body continuously strengthens it. The enhancement is calculated as follows:

Body Enhancement (%) = Level ÷ 10

For example, 1,000 Levels yield a +100% enhancement. This means the body's cells become twice as strong, efficient, precise, resilient, and fast. At one million Levels, cellular enhancement reaches a point where the being could theoretically sustain itself indefinitely — aging ceases to be a limiting factor. Memory remains a separate challenge, though the enhanced neurons also shrink in size, allowing for greater information density and storage capacity.

Attribute Multipliers

Every attribute that Mana can apply to Matter carries its own multiplier, and no two multipliers are identical. The more complex or powerful the attribute, the higher its multiplier. Some examples:

  • Antigravity: x = g (e.g. 9.81 m/s²)
  • Push / Shoot: x = m/s (the desired velocity)
  • Luck: x = ±%
  • Erase: x = 10⁷

Aura

Aura is to Magic what heat is to electronics — a residual byproduct of spells being cast. When a spell is performed, an aura forms around the caster. Its visual appearance is unique to the soul producing it, but it is always nearly transparent with a faint glow. The thickness of the aura scales with the caster's power: 1cm / 1000LE so e.g. 50LE = 0,05cm of Aura

Limitations of Mana

Mana is bound by no laws of nature — no speed of light, no drag, no physical resistance. If accelerated, it will continue gaining speed indefinitely until it makes contact with solid pos/neg Matter, at which point it nests within it. Mana cannot nest in Darkmatter, as Darkmatter lacks the structural form required: it exists as an undifferentiated soup of dark-atoms, without solid definition.

Mana can only be stored within solid Matter. Outside of a solid medium, it simply drifts freely and cannot interact with Matter at all — the atoms in non-solid states move too rapidly for Mana to anchor to them.

Two things lie entirely beyond Mana's reach. The first is teleportation: nothing can move from one place to another without traversing the distance between them. An object could be reconstructed elsewhere, but it would not be the same object. The second is breaking the fabric of space itself — Mana cannot pierce layers of reality(verses, bubbles, etc).

Anam

Anam is the positive counterpart to Mana. Where Mana defies the laws of nature entirely, Anam is fully subject to them. It has a defined minimum size — a "perfect" sphere with a radius of 2 Planck lengths: 1 Planck length for the hollow interior and 1 for the outer wall. It has mass (10 grams per dm³) and a hard speed limit. It can also seen with the naked eye. It is also very bouncy and cannot pop, so reforming is not a problem.

That speed limit is of particular note: if Anam exceeds its maximum velocity, it explodes. The formula governing this limit is:

Max speed = 5 m/s × volume⁻⁰·¹

For example: 5 × 5⁻⁰·¹ ≈ 4.2567 m/s

Anam interacts with everything. It can be pushed; it pushes back. Gravity acts on it normally. In appearance, it is a lazy, semi-transparent, faintly violet bubble — light, soft, and just solid enough that passing through it requires a gentle push. Anam cannot pop besides going too fast.

Explosions and Holes in Reality

When Anam explodes or implodes(then it also explodes), it tears a hole in a layer, in reality — a rift into the Verse or the Anambubble layer in which the explosion occurred. Because the hole is torn into space rather than into Matter, it does not lead anywhere predetermined. Instead, it connects to a random Verse or Anambubble in the same layerplane.

These holes can be used for travel, but only within the same plane of existence. All Verses occupy the lowest level — there is no outside. Anambubbles that are direct children of a Verse (contained within it) can only connect to other bubbles within that same Verse. Bubbles nested inside other bubbles can only connect to bubbles sharing the same parent bubble.

Casting with Anam

It is possible to cast with Anam, but the method is fundamentally different from Mana-based casting. The soul has no innate understanding of Anam — it cannot be guided by thought or intent alone. The only valid method is to speak the complete spell aloud, in full.

The only thing that can be cast with Anam is a controlled explosion: a precisely directed rupture, where the caster determines the destination of the hole, its size, and how many layers of reality it tears through. The Level cost of such a spell is:

Anam Spell Level = Mana Volume (cm³) × sum(1.1(conversion to Anam) + x m³(size) + push-speed(m/s) + x(layers, e.g. 2) + hours )

Mana attributes can also be added to Anam, applying to it in the same way they would to any other Matter.
